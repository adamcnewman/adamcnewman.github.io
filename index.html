3D Web Simulation: Stern-Gerlach Experiment

This project spawned from a remark that the professor of my Modern and Quantum Physics class made while we were learning about quantum properties of particles. She loves to incorporate 2D web simulations into her lectures, and she mentioned that the physics community would benefit from having a good simulation of this experiment, since one isn't really available. Since I've found these simulations to be immensely helpful in understanding difficult physics concepts, for our semester project, I decided... why don't I make that simulation!

The main goal of this project is to use 3D web technologies to facilitate a more fun and immersive learning experience for students who are learning about both this experiment, and quantum properties of particles. This is still a work in progress, and I expect to complete it by April 2022.

Technologies Used

While researching this experiment, and wondering how I could recreate it in a new and exciting way, I came across the THREE.js Library. It hit me - I could take this project a step further and make it 3D! I ended up deciding to make a 3D world using the THREE.js library, and opted to use Blender to create some of the more complex 3D models.
